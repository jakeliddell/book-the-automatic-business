# 9.1 Process Archaeology

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 9, at the Process Archaeology method
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
An archaeological dig where the excavation pit is the silhouette of a head; tagged artefacts sit on a finds table while a robot in a pith helmet takes notes.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A proper archaeological dig site: string grid stretched over the excavation, trowels, brushes, a wheelbarrow. The excavation pit, seen from above at a slight angle, is unmistakably the silhouette of a human head in profile. Diggers work carefully inside it. On a folding finds table nearby, excavated artefacts with museum tags: an odd mechanical widget tagged THE WORKAROUND (ANCIENT), a scroll tagged THE EXCEPTION, a small tablet tagged THE BIT EVERYONE GETS WRONG. To one side, a robot in a pith helmet sits on a crate taking meticulous notes in a field journal.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): THE WORKAROUND (ANCIENT), THE EXCEPTION, THE BIT EVERYONE GETS WRONG on the artefact tags. Exactly three tags — no other tags, stamps, or words anywhere in the image
