# 8.2 The Leaver

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 8, at 'knowledge walks out of the door'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (3:2)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
An employee leaves carrying a cardboard box with a huge jigsaw piece sticking out; the office building behind has a matching jigsaw-shaped hole in its wall.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: An office building on the left. An employee, seen from the front, walks AWAY from it toward the right edge of the frame, the building receding behind her. She carries a cardboard box of desk belongings — a plant, a mug, a framed photo — and sticking up out of the box, an unlabelled jigsaw puzzle piece the size of a paving slab. In the building's brick wall behind her: a jigsaw-piece-shaped hole, exactly matching the piece in her box. At the door, a receptionist waves goodbye. Played completely straight, slightly melancholy. No labels on anything — the image explains itself.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): ONLY a small banner in an office window reading GOOD LUCK SUSAN! — no other words, names, labels, or annotations anywhere in the image.
