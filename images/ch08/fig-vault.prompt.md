# 8.1 The Vault

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-second-brain
- Placement: Ch 8, at 'stored in the worst possible place'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
Cutaway of the owner's head as a bank vault full of filing cabinets; staff queue at the ear holding deli tickets under a NOW SERVING sign.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A large profile portrait of a business owner's head, drawn in cutaway. Inside the skull: a bank vault interior — rows of filing cabinets, stacked ledgers, and a round wall safe with a combination dial, its door labelled WHY CLIENT X REALLY STAYS. Outside the head, at the ear, a patient queue of employees, each holding a small paper deli-counter ticket. Mounted above the ear, a small sign.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): WHY CLIENT X REALLY STAYS on the safe door; the sign reading NOW SERVING: No. 47
