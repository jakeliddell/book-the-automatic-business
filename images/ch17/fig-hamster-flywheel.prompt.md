# 17.1 Hamster Wheel, Flywheel

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-flywheel
- Placement: Ch 17, at the hamster wheel/flywheel passage
- Aspect: Very wide landscape (2:1), two panels
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
The owner sprints in a hamster wheel labelled PANIC MARKETING; beside it a massive flywheel with five labelled stages turns on its own from one light fingertip push.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Wide landscape, 16:9.

Scene: Two panels. LEFT PANEL: the business owner sprinting flat-out inside a giant hamster wheel labelled PANIC MARKETING, social posts and flyers flying off it in all directions, sweat drops — and cobwebs already forming on the wheel's lower half where it stopped last time. RIGHT PANEL: a massive, heavy industrial flywheel, clearly spinning steadily on its own (motion lines), and around it, EXACTLY five small rectangular signs on posts, arranged clockwise around the wheel like stations, each sign written HORIZONTALLY (straight, never curved, never rotated), in clockwise order starting from the top: CREATE, then CAPTURE, then NURTURE, then DELIGHT, then REFER. Each word appears exactly once. Curved arrows on the wheel rim show the clockwise motion between the signs. The owner stands beside it giving it the lightest of pushes with one extended index finger, cup of tea in the other hand.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): PANIC MARKETING on the hamster wheel; CREATE, CAPTURE, NURTURE, DELIGHT, REFER around the flywheel rim
