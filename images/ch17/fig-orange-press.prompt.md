# 17.2 The Orange Press

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 17, at 'more juice from the same orange'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
One orange labelled ONE GOOD IDEA in a Victorian screw-press; from its spouts, labelled bottles fill with an article, five posts, an email sequence and a video, while an AI robot turns the screw.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A magnificent Victorian cast-iron screw-press on a wooden table. In its basket: a single orange with a small tag reading ONE GOOD IDEA. From four spouts at the base, juice streams into four labelled glass bottles: AN ARTICLE, FIVE POSTS, AN EMAIL SEQUENCE, A VIDEO. A friendly AI robot, sleeves rolled up on its metal arms, turns the big screw handle with visible effort.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): ONE GOOD IDEA on the orange tag; AN ARTICLE, FIVE POSTS, AN EMAIL SEQUENCE, A VIDEO on the bottles
