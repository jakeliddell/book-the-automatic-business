# 19.2 The Helpful Scheduler

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Optional
- Placement: Ch 19, at the AI scheduler critique
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
An immaculate butler robot files a document stamped THE MOST IMPORTANT THING into a drawer labelled A WEEK ON THURSDAY as the owner reaches after it too late.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: An immaculate butler robot — bow tie, white gloves over metal hands, serene expression — sliding a document into an open filing drawer with evident satisfaction. The document is rubber-stamped in large letters: THE MOST IMPORTANT THING. The drawer's brass label frame reads: A WEEK ON THURSDAY. Behind the robot, the business owner lunges after the document, one hand outstretched, a fraction too late. On the wall, a perfectly colour-coded calendar, absurdly neat.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): THE MOST IMPORTANT THING on the document stamp; A WEEK ON THURSDAY on the drawer label
