# 15.1 The Beach Test

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core — bookend of 1.2
- Placement: Ch 15, at 'from a beach, on your phone, in ten seconds'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (3:2)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
The same beach deckchair as Chapter One, but the desk phone is gone: a smartphone in the sand shows seven green dots, and the old phone cord ends in a neat unplugged coil.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: The same quiet beach and deckchair as the Chapter One holiday image. This time: no desk telephone. A smartphone is propped casually in the sand against the deckchair leg, its screen showing a simple dashboard of seven glowing dots, all filled (indicating green). The cocktail is half-drunk and relaxed. In the foreground, the old spiral telephone cord lies in a neat coil in the sand, its plug end clearly unplugged, with a tiny flag planted in the coil. The owner's legs are visible, crossed and at rest.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): ALL FINE on the phone screen header; tiny flag optionally blank
