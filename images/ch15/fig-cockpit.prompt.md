# 15.2 The Cockpit

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-dashboard

## Alt text
A light-aircraft cockpit with exactly seven big gauges labelled with business numbers, all in the green; behind the pilot, a forty-gauge panel under a dust sheet labelled EVERYTHING ELSE.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: The interior of a light aircraft cockpit, pilot's view over the shoulder. The instrument panel holds exactly seven big, friendly, readable gauges, each labelled: PIPELINE, CASH, OVERDUE, ON-TIME, LEADS, CHURN, HOURS SAVED — needles comfortably in the safe zone. Through the windscreen far below: a tidy little business — buildings, delivery van, tiny people — running along fine. Behind the pilot's seat, a second instrument panel crowded with forty tiny gauges, covered by a draped dust sheet with a hand-lettered tag.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): the seven gauge labels as given; EVERYTHING ELSE on the dust-sheet tag
