# 6.2 The Ninety-Minute Machine

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Optional — case study
- Placement: Ch 6, at the proposal-automation story
- Aspect: Very wide landscape (2:1), two panels
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A dusty wall-sized Rube Goldberg blueprint tagged NEVER LAUNCHED beside a shoebox-sized machine with one lever tagged LIVE IN 90 MINUTES.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Wide landscape, 16:9.

Scene: Two panels. LEFT: a wall-sized blueprint of an absurd Rube Goldberg contraption — funnels, conditional gates, bells, twelve numbered stages — pinned up, gathering dust and one cobweb, with a paper tag hanging off it. RIGHT: a modest shoebox-sized machine on a desk with a single big lever and two slots labelled PROPOSAL and SENT, a small proud tag tied to it.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): NEVER LAUNCHED on the left tag; PROPOSAL and SENT on the slots; LIVE IN 90 MINUTES on the right tag
