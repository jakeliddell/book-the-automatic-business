# 6.1 The Prospector's Map

- Class: Diagram (numbered figure)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-value-effort
- Placement: Ch 6, at the value/effort matrix
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
The value-versus-effort matrix drawn as a treasure map with a gold seam marked START HERE and a graveyard corner where good intentions go to die.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients. Keep the layout clear and instructional: the drawing must read as a diagram at a glance.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A hand-drawn treasure map divided into four quarter territories by two crossed dotted trails, with axis banners along the edges reading VALUE (vertical) and EFFORT (horizontal). Top-left territory: a glittering gold seam in rock, a pickaxe planted in it, and a wooden sign reading START HERE. Top-right territory: tall mountains with a sign reading THE FLAGSHIPS — WORTH THE CLIMB, LATER. Bottom-left: a small pleasant meadow with a sign reading NICE, I SUPPOSE. Bottom-right: a tiny fenced graveyard with leaning tombstones, one legible: BUILT IN A MONTH, SAVED 20 MIN/YR; the gate sign reads WHERE GOOD INTENTIONS GO TO DIE.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): VALUE and EFFORT on the axis banners; START HERE; THE FLAGSHIPS — WORTH THE CLIMB, LATER; NICE, I SUPPOSE; WHERE GOOD INTENTIONS GO TO DIE; tombstone BUILT IN A MONTH, SAVED 20 MIN/YR
