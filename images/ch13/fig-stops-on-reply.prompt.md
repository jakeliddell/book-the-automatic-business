# 13.2 It Stops the Instant You Reply

- Class: Diagram (numbered figure)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-comms-timing
- Placement: Ch 13, at the timing rules
- Aspect: Very wide landscape (2:1)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
Scheduled paper planes fly along a timeline until the client's reply crosses it; the remaining planes brake mid-air, one folding itself back into a flat sheet.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients. Keep the layout clear and instructional: the drawing must read as a diagram at a glance.

Format: Wide landscape, 16:9.

Scene: A horizontal timeline drawn as a long dotted flight path. Along it, three paper planes have launched at marked intervals from small catapult posts labelled DAY 1, DAY 3, DAY 7. From the right side, a different paper plane labelled REPLY flies back across the line. At that instant: the remaining outbound planes brake hard in mid-air — cartoon skid marks in the sky — and the last one is politely unfolding itself back into a flat sheet of paper.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): DAY 1, DAY 3, DAY 7 on the catapult posts; REPLY on the returning plane
