# M4 — Ch 6 margin arrow

- Class: Marginalia (one-off, never reused)
- Placement: Pointing to 'A bad process, automated, is just a bad process that runs faster.'
- Print: B&W, transparent background after knockout

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black ink pen mark on a plain pure-white background, nothing else in the frame. Loose, confident, single-pass stroke, as if drawn quickly in the margin of a book by the author. Slight wobble, natural ink weight variation. No colour, no shading, no border. (Post-production: white background knocked out to transparency; placed in the print margin at the exact line noted.)

Mark: A straight, emphatic arrow from the margin, drawn with pressure — the shaft thickens toward the head. Words at the tail.

Hand-lettered words (casual, quick handwriting — match the Patrick Hand feel of the figure lettering): read that twice
