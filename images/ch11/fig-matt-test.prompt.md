## Alt text
An old-school salesman leans back happily on the phone while pipeline cards move themselves across a board behind him.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A small spot illustration. An old-school salesman — shirt sleeves, tie loosened, decades of experience in his face — leans back in his chair, feet up on the desk, telephone receiver cradled to his ear, genuinely smiling. Behind him on the wall, a pipeline board where cards are visibly moving themselves along (motion lines), no hands touching them. A speech bubble from the salesman.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): speech bubble reading I DON'T USUALLY LIKE CRMs.
