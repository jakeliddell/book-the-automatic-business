# 11.1 The CRM Graveyard

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 11, at 'the expensive graveyard it became last time'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A moonlit graveyard of dead CRMs with epitaph tombstones; the owner stands with a shovel and a new boxed CRM, hesitating.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A small moonlit graveyard on a hill, iron fence, crescent moon. Leaning tombstones with legible epitaphs: CRM 2016 — FILLED IN TWICE; CRM 2019 — DIED OF EMPTY FIELDS; and one small squat stone, faintly glowing from within, reading THE SPREADSHEET — SURVIVED THEM ALL. In the foreground, the business owner stands holding a shovel in one hand and a fresh software box under the other arm, labelled CRM 2026, visibly hesitating.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): the three epitaphs as given; CRM 2026 on the box
