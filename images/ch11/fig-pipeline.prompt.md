# 11.2 The Board That Works

- Class: Diagram (numbered figure)
- Priority: Core — redraw of old fig-pipeline
- Placement: Ch 11, at the pipeline description
- Aspect: Very wide landscape (2:1)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
Lead cards ride rails through four pipeline stages, nudged by tiny robot arms; one sleeping card is approached by a feather labelled GENTLE NUDGE.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients. Keep the layout clear and instructional: the drawing must read as a diagram at a glance.

Format: Wide landscape, 16:9.

Scene: A left-to-right pipeline board drawn as a small rail yard: four station columns labelled NEW, CALL BOOKED, PROPOSAL, SIGNED. Lead cards — small rectangles with a name line and a photo corner — ride little rail trolleys between the stations. Tiny mechanical robot arms on posts nudge the cards along. At the PROPOSAL station one card is asleep (nightcap, small Z Z Z), and descending toward it from above, held by another robot arm, a single large feather with a small tag.

Lettering: the ONLY words in the entire image are the four station signs — NEW, CALL BOOKED, PROPOSAL, SIGNED — and the feather tag reading GENTLE NUDGE. No captions along the bottom, no labels on the cards or trolleys, no other text of any kind
