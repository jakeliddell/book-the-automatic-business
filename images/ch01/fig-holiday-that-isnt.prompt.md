# 1.2 The Holiday That Isn't

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Optional — pairs with 15.1 The Beach Test
- Placement: Ch 1, at 'the holiday that is not really a holiday'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (3:2)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A beach deckchair with an office desk phone half-buried in the sand beside it, its cord running back over the dunes; the owner reaches for it.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A quiet beach. A deckchair with a folded towel and an untouched cocktail with a paper umbrella. Half-buried in the sand next to the deckchair: a full office desk telephone, mid-ring (motion lines), its long spiral cord running away across the sand dunes to the horizon. The owner, in holiday clothes, is leaning over from the deckchair, mid-reach for the receiver.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): none
