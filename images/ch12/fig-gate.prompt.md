# 12.1 The Gate

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 12, at the gate guardrail
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A nightclub bouncer labelled THE GATE turns away an incomplete deal card at a velvet rope while a complete card breezes past.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A nightclub entrance with a velvet rope line. A huge bouncer in a suit with an earpiece stands with one palm raised, holding a clipboard labelled THE GATE. Being refused: an anthropomorphised deal card with little legs, one corner visibly torn off where its details should be, pleading with clasped hands. Behind it in the queue, a pristine complete card struts confidently past the lifted rope — its face shows only neat filled-in form lines (wavy scribble lines, no readable words). A sign on the rope post.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): THE GATE on the clipboard; the rope sign reading NO INVOICE DETAILS, NO ENTRY.
