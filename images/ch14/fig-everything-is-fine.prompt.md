# 14.1 Everything Is Fine

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core — style-test image
- Placement: Ch 14, at 'they never tell you when they are broken'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
An office robot with a sparking arm and glitching screen gives a big thumbs up; its mug reads WORLD'S OKAYEST AUTOMATION and the calendar says LAST CHECKED: Q1.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: An office robot sitting at a tidy desk, facing the viewer, giving a big confident thumbs up with one hand. Its other arm is visibly sparking (jagged spark lines), a thin curl of smoke rises from its shoulder joint, and its chest screen shows a glitching, scrambled graph. On the desk, a coffee mug with printed text. On the wall behind, a calendar with a note scrawled across it.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): WORLD'S OKAYEST AUTOMATION on the mug; LAST CHECKED: Q1 on the calendar
