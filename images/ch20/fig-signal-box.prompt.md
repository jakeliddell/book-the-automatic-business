# 20.3 The Signal Box

- Class: Diagram (numbered figure)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-replacement-map (supersedes the switchboard concept)
- Placement: Ch 20, at the You Replacement Map
- Aspect: Landscape, 4:3
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A railway junction: tasks arrive as goods wagons on tracks that once all led to a platform marked ME; from a signal box the owner switches the points so most tracks divert to sidings marked SYSTEM, TEAMMATE and KEYSTONE, with two tracks deliberately left running to ME, their lever ribbon-tied MINE, BY CHOICE.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients. Keep the layout clear and instructional: the drawing must read as a diagram at a glance.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A railway junction seen from slightly above. From the left, several train tracks stream in, each carrying a little goods wagon loaded with paperwork. The tracks converge at a set of points, then fan out to the right toward four destinations: a small station platform labelled ME, and three sidings labelled SYSTEM, TEAMMATE, KEYSTONE. Most points are switched so the wagons visibly curve away toward the three sidings. Only two tracks still run to the ME platform. Above the junction, the business owner sits in a raised wooden signal box, calmly pulling one of a row of tall points levers. One lever is tied with a neat ribbon bow, a small tag hanging from it.

Lettering: the only words in the image are ME, SYSTEM, TEAMMATE, KEYSTONE on the platform and siding signs, and the ribbon tag reading MINE, BY CHOICE. No other text of any kind.
