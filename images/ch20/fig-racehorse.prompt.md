# 20.2 The Racehorse

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Optional
- Placement: Ch 20, at 'the way a racehorse pulls a plough badly'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (3:2)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A thoroughbred in racing silks harnessed to a plough leaves a wildly zigzag furrow, while a carthorse labelled THE INTEGRATOR ploughs dead-straight lines in the next field.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A ploughing field in two halves divided by a low hedge. Near side: a magnificent thoroughbred racehorse, still wearing racing silks and a number cloth, harnessed to a wooden plough — rearing slightly, wild-eyed, its furrow behind it a chaotic zigzag with loops. Far side: a heavy, placid carthorse harnessed to an identical plough, its furrows dead straight and evenly spaced, glancing over the hedge with one unimpressed eye. A small painted board leans on the hedge.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): the number cloth on the racehorse reading THE VISIONARY; the board reading THE INTEGRATOR, placed on the carthorse's side of the hedge (no arrow)
