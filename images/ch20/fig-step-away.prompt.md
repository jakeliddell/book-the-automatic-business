# 20.1 Step Away From the Machine

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 20, at 'the danger is that you will climb back in'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A gleaming machine with every dial in the green; the owner in fresh overalls tiptoes toward it with an enormous spanner while the team watches, arms folded.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A gleaming, humming machine filling the right of the frame, every gauge needle happily in the safe zone, a soft steam whistle of contentment. Sneaking toward it on exaggerated tiptoe from the left: the business owner, wearing brand-new overalls with visible fold creases (price tag still on), carrying an enormous spanner behind his back and glancing over his shoulder. Behind him, three team members stand in a row, arms folded, eyebrows raised. A small brass plaque on the machine.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): the plaque reading RUNNING FINE. DO NOT "IMPROVE".
