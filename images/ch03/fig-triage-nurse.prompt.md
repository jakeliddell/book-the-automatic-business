# 3.1 The Triage Nurse

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-triage
- Placement: Ch 3, at the hospital triage opening
- Aspect: Very wide landscape (2:1)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A triage nurse sorts a queue of anthropomorphised tasks toward three doors: HUMAN, AUTOMATION and AI.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Wide landscape, 16:9.

Scene: A hospital corridor. A nurse sits at a desk labelled TRIAGE, pointing firmly with one arm, entirely unimpressed. Queuing in front of the desk, a line of anthropomorphised tasks with little arms and legs: a six-page rambling letter dragging its trailing pages behind it, an invoice with a bandaged corner, a customer-complaint envelope that is literally on fire. Beyond the desk, three doors in a row: one marked HUMAN with a comfortable armchair visible through it, one marked AUTOMATION with a conveyor belt running through it, one marked AI with a keen little robot sitting at a desk inside.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): TRIAGE on the desk; HUMAN, AUTOMATION, AI on the three doors
