# 3.2 The Chain

- Class: Diagram (numbered figure)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 3, at 'most tasks are really a chain'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (3:2)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
One parcel labelled DEAL WITH THE ENQUIRY pulled apart into four sausage links stamped A, AI, H, A.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients. Keep the layout clear and instructional: the drawing must read as a diagram at a glance.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A single fat paper parcel tied with string, being pulled apart from both ends by two pairs of hands. As it comes apart it reveals it was really a chain of four sausage-shaped linked segments, still joined. Each link carries one big rubber-stamped letter: the first A, the second AI, the third H, the fourth A. The H link is drawn slightly warmer: a small heart-shaped wax seal on it.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): DEAL WITH THE ENQUIRY on the parcel wrapper; A, AI, H, A stamped on the four links
