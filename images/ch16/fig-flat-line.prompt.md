# 16.2 The Flat Line

- Class: Diagram (numbered figure)
- Priority: Core — redraw of old fig-scaling
- Placement: Ch 16, at the scaling payoff
- Aspect: Wide landscape (4:3)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A line chart where a tiny figure climbs the steepening WORKLOAD curve carrying a client, while another strolls the flat WITH SYSTEMS line as little machines carry the clients behind him.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients. Keep the layout clear and instructional: the drawing must read as a diagram at a glance.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A hand-drawn line chart. Horizontal axis labelled CLIENTS, vertical axis labelled YOUR WORKLOAD. Two lines: one curving steeply upward, one staying almost flat. On the steep line, a tiny exhausted figure climbs it like a mountain slope, carrying a client piggyback, with more tiny clients queueing at the base. On the flat line, the same figure strolls along it like a boardwalk, hands in pockets, whistling (a few music notes), while behind him a line of little wheeled machines each carries a happy client.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): CLIENTS and YOUR WORKLOAD on the axes; WITHOUT SYSTEMS on the steep line; WITH SYSTEMS on the flat line
