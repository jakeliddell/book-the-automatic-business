# 16.1 Lego, Not Bespoke

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 16, at 'systemise the predictable, humanise the exceptional'
- Aspect: Very wide landscape (2:1), two panels
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A craftsman hand-carves one ornate chair with clients queueing out the door; beside it, the same service as a kit of numbered parts, three chairs built, the craftsman adding one carved flourish labelled THE 10%.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Wide landscape, 16:9.

Scene: Two panels. LEFT PANEL: a craftsman hand-carving a single wildly ornate chair, wood shavings up to his knees, mallet and chisel, sweat drops — while a queue of impatient clients stretches out the workshop door, one tapping a wristwatch. RIGHT PANEL: the same workshop transformed: flat-pack style numbered standard parts (labelled 1, 2, 3) snapping together with motion lines, three finished chairs already standing in a row — and the craftsman, relaxed, adding one small hand-carved flourish to the top of each chair, his chisel poised, a small tag hanging from the flourish.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): part numbers 1, 2, 3; THE 10% on the flourish tag
