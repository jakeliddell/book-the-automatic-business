# I.1 The Cage

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Optional (frontispiece candidate)
- Placement: Introduction, near 'started becoming your cage'
- Aspect: Square-ish (4:5 to 1:1)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A birdcage containing a complete tiny office with the owner working inside; the cage door stands wide open.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: A large Victorian birdcage on a stand. Inside it, a complete miniature office: a desk piled with paperwork, a filing cabinet, a ringing telephone, and a business owner typing intently at a laptop. The cage door stands wide open. The owner has not noticed. Empty white space all around the cage.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): none required (optionally a tiny luggage tag on the cage reading YOUR BUSINESS)
