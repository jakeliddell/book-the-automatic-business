# 2.1 The Pit Wall

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core — replaces old fig-technician-architect
- Placement: Ch 2, at the F1 mechanic/pit wall passage
- Aspect: Very wide landscape (2:1), two panels
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
Two panels: a mechanic buried under a car labelled THE BUSINESS with tasks raining down; the same person on the pit wall with headset and telemetry screens as the car races past.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Wide landscape, 16:9.

Scene: Two panels side by side, separated by a hand-drawn gutter line. LEFT PANEL: a Formula 1 mechanic lying half under a racing car, head hidden, oil drips, spanners and paperwork raining down on him from above. The car body is labelled THE BUSINESS. RIGHT PANEL: the same man seated on the pit wall, wearing a headset, calm, studying two telemetry screens with graphs, while the same car flashes past below at speed (speed lines).

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): THE BUSINESS on the car; across the bottom of the gutter, SAME PERSON. DIFFERENT JOB.
