# 2.2 A Broken Process, With a Salary

- Class: Cartoon (unnumbered)
- Priority: Core
- Placement: Ch 2, at 'a broken process with a salary attached'
- Aspect: Wide landscape (3:2)
- Print: pure B&W

## Alt text
A machine leaks paperwork from a hole while a cheerful new hire catches it in a bucket priced at 28,000 pounds a year; nobody fixes the hole.

## Prompt
Hand-drawn black-and-white editorial cartoon illustration. Fine ink pen linework with dense crosshatching and stipple shading, on a plain pure-white background. Bold hand-lettered labels in clean marker capitals sit INSIDE the drawing, on the objects themselves. One physical metaphor, played completely straight; wry, deadpan humour. No colour, no border, no title or caption text outside the artwork, no photorealism, no digital gradients.

Format: Landscape, 4:3.

Scene: An industrial machine with pipes and dials, working away, with a ragged hole in its side. Out of the hole spills a stream of envelopes, invoices and lead forms. A cheerful new employee, wearing a lanyard, holds a bucket underneath the stream, catching the paperwork. A large price tag hangs from the bucket handle. The hole itself is completely ignored; a roll of repair tape sits unused on the floor nearby.

Lettering (must appear exactly as written, hand-lettered inside the drawing): NEW HIRE on the lanyard; £28,000/YR on the bucket price tag
