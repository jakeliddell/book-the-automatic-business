# Afterword: A Letter From Me to You {.unnumbered}

You have reached the end, so let me put down the author's voice and write this last page as what I actually am, which is one of you.

I have founded and managed three small businesses of my own. And for the last twenty years, the people on the other side of my desk have been small business owners: your problems, your two-in-the-morning worries, your quiet certainty that there must be a better way. This book exists because of them, and because of the younger me. It is the book I wish someone had pressed into my hands at the start: the one I could have followed, step by step, instead of learning every lesson in it the expensive way.

And it was written just as much for the readers who do not think of themselves as technical. If that is you, I want the last thing you take from this book to be the words of the coach Dan Sullivan: who, not how. You do not need to become the person who knows how to do everything in these pages. You need to find the people, and now the machines, that know how, and point them at the right problems. That has never been easier than it is today. Figure it out. Get it done.

If you are feeling overwhelmed, or simply stuck on where to start, drop me a message through jakeliddell.com. I read them, and I will do my best to point you in the right direction.

Because here is what I believe, and it is the real reason this book exists. People like you, who were brave enough to build something of your own, are the backbone of this country's prosperity. When your business becomes steadier, saner and stronger, the good does not stop with you: it flows to your family, your team, their families, and the whole community that stands around them. You dared to try, and you deserve to get back something better than the longest hours and the lowest wage in the building.

Fortune favours the brave. Go and do what only you can do.

Jake Liddell.
